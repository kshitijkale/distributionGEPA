# Know When You're Wrong: Aligning Confidence with Correctness for LLM Error Detection

**Section:** 7.5 — Calibration and Verbalized Confidence

**Authors:** (2026)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2603.06604](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06604)

**Venue:** 2026

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Trains models to align their confidence expressions with actual correctness — relevant to understanding whether and how distributional signals can help models (and reflectors) identify errors.

---

## Key Concept

Rather than post-hoc calibration techniques, this paper trains models to better predict when they are wrong. This creates models whose uncertainty signals (both textual and distributional) are more informative.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Context for error detection:** Our DAL identifies uncertainty patterns that may indicate errors. This paper's findings about what makes confidence-correctness alignment work could inform which distributional features are most diagnostic.

2. **Future direction:** If models could be fine-tuned to have better-aligned confidence (per this paper), the DAL's distributional features would become even more informative. However, this requires weight modification — outside GEPA's prompt-only scope.

3. **Baseline calibration context:** When interpreting our calibration measurements, models trained with this approach would be expected to show different distributional patterns than standard models.
