# Decomposing Uncertainty for LLMs through Input Clarification Ensembling

**Section:** 7.7 — Uncertainty Decomposition

**Authors:** Yuxin Hou et al.

**Paper:** [arXiv:2311.08718](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.08718)

**Venue:** ICML 2024

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Distinguishes **aleatoric uncertainty** (irreducible — ambiguous input) from **epistemic uncertainty** (reducible — model lacks knowledge). These two types demand fundamentally different prompt mutations, making the decomposition valuable for informed reflection.

---

## Key Concept: Uncertainty Decomposition

| Type | Source | Example | Prompt Fix |
|------|--------|---------|------------|
| **Aleatoric** | Ambiguous input | "What is the bank?" (river or finance?) | Clarify disambiguation — "interpret 'bank' as financial institution" |
| **Epistemic** | Model ignorance | Model doesn't know the capital of a new country | Add factual context — "Note: the capital of X is Y" |

**Method:** Generate paraphrased/clarified versions of the input (an "ensemble" of inputs). If the model's answer changes across paraphrases → aleatoric uncertainty. If the answer is consistently wrong across paraphrases → epistemic uncertainty.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Informs reflector reasoning:** If the DAL can identify whether uncertainty at a forking token is aleatoric vs. epistemic, the reflector can propose more targeted mutations:
   - **Aleatoric:** Add disambiguation instructions to the prompt
   - **Epistemic:** Add relevant factual context or reasoning strategies

2. **Potential DAL feature:** The DAL could flag the likely uncertainty type based on distributional signatures:
   - **Aleatoric:** High entropy with semantically unrelated competitors (different interpretations)
   - **Epistemic:** High entropy with semantically related competitors (different answers to the same interpretation)

3. **Connects to H4 (emergent differentiation):** We hypothesize the reflector will spontaneously produce different mutation strategies for different distributional signatures. If aleatoric vs. epistemic patterns lead to different reflector behaviors, that's evidence for H4.

## Connection to Other Papers

- **Anatomy of Uncertainty (7.7.3):** Extends to a three-way decomposition (input ambiguity, knowledge gaps, decoding randomness)
- **Semantic Uncertainty (7.3.1):** Semantic entropy conflates aleatoric and epistemic; this paper separates them
- **Beyond Next Token Probabilities (7.6.4):** Distribution shape may correlate with uncertainty type

---

## Limitations

- Input clarification ensembling adds cost (multiple paraphrases needed)
- The decomposition is approximate — real failure modes often mix aleatoric and epistemic
- May be too expensive to include in the DAL pipeline; more useful as a conceptual framework for analyzing results
