# DoLa: Decoding by Contrasting Layers Improves Factuality and Reasoning

**Section:** 7.6 — Contrastive and Distribution-Based Methods

**Authors:** Yung-Sung Chuang et al.

**Paper:** [arXiv:2309.03883](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03883)

**Venue:** ICLR 2024

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

DoLa demonstrates that **layer-wise distributional contrasts** detect when the model is relying on surface patterns vs. deep factual knowledge. For open-weight models, this provides an additional diagnostic feature beyond output logprobs.

---

## Key Concept

1. During generation, compare the output distribution from the final layer with distributions from earlier layers
2. The contrast identifies tokens where factual knowledge (emerging in later layers) overrides surface-level patterns (present in earlier layers)
3. Amplifying these contrasts during decoding improves factuality

```
logits_final = logits_layer_N - logits_layer_k  (k < N)
```

Where layer k is selected dynamically based on where the biggest distributional shift occurs.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Additional diagnostic for open-weight models:** For experiments using vLLM with Llama 3 70B, we could compute layer-wise contrasts to identify tokens where the model's factual knowledge disagrees with its surface-level patterns. This provides a richer diagnostic than output entropy alone.

2. **Detecting surface-pattern failures:** If the model's final-layer distribution differs significantly from its mid-layer distribution at a particular token, it suggests the model is "overriding" its initial instinct — which could indicate either correction (good) or second-guessing (bad).

3. **Not in primary DAL design:** Layer-wise contrasts require hidden state access (not just logprobs), making this more restrictive than our primary top-k logprob approach. Consider as an optional extension for open-weight experiments.

## Connection to Other Papers

- **Contrastive Decoding (7.6.1):** DoLa applies the same contrastive principle within a single model across layers
- **CCS (7.6.3):** Both use internal model representations; CCS uses probes, DoLa uses layer contrasts
- **Semantic Entropy Probes (7.3.3):** Also accesses hidden states for richer information
