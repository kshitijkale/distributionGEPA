# Semantic Entropy Probes: Robust and Cheap Hallucination Detection in LLMs

**Section:** 7.3 — Semantic Entropy and Meaning-Level Uncertainty

**Authors:** Jannik Kossen et al. (Oxford)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2406.15927](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.15927)

**Venue:** NeurIPS 2024

**Code:** [github.com/OATML/semantic-entropy-probes](https://github.com/OATML/semantic-entropy-probes)

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Demonstrates that **linear probes on hidden states** can predict semantic entropy from a single forward pass. This implies distributional information is encoded in the model's internal representations, not just output logits — a deeper validation of the distributional information hypothesis.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Single-pass estimation:** Instead of sampling 5-10 outputs and running NLI clustering (expensive), train a linear probe on hidden states that predicts semantic entropy directly.

2. **Cheap and robust:** The probe approach is orders of magnitude cheaper than full semantic entropy computation while maintaining strong hallucination detection performance.

3. **Distributional information is in hidden states:** The fact that a simple linear probe can extract uncertainty information from hidden states means this information is readily available in the model's representations — it's not an emergent property of multiple samples.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Validates the distributional information hypothesis:** If hidden states encode semantic entropy, then output logprobs (which are derived from hidden states) also encode rich uncertainty information. This supports our claim that logprob-based diagnostics carry information beyond text traces.

2. **Potential efficiency improvement:** For open-weight models, we could use semantic entropy probes as an alternative to computing entropy from logprobs — getting meaning-level uncertainty cheaply. However, this requires probe training, which adds setup complexity.

3. **Future direction:** If the DAL proves valuable but logprob-based features are insufficient, hidden-state probes could provide richer distributional features. This is beyond our current scope but worth noting.

## Limitations for Our Context

- Requires open-weight models with hidden state access — more restrictive than logprobs
- Probe training requires labeled data — doesn't fit GEPA's general-purpose framework
- Adds implementation complexity beyond our DAL scope
