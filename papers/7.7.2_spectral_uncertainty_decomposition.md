# Fine-Grained Uncertainty Decomposition: A Spectral Approach

**Section:** 7.7 — Uncertainty Decomposition

**Authors:** (2025)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2509.22272](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.22272)

**Venue:** 2025

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Represents the theoretical frontier of uncertainty decomposition. Uses spectral methods (eigenvalue analysis of uncertainty matrices) for fine-grained decomposition beyond the binary aleatoric/epistemic split.

---

## Key Concept

Uses spectral analysis (eigendecomposition) of uncertainty-related matrices to provide finer-grained decomposition of different uncertainty sources. Goes beyond the binary aleatoric/epistemic distinction to identify multiple independent sources of uncertainty.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Theoretical awareness:** This is the most principled approach to uncertainty decomposition, but likely too computationally expensive for GEPA's budget-constrained optimization.

2. **Future direction:** If the simpler uncertainty features in our DAL prove valuable, spectral decomposition could provide a principled upgrade path for richer distributional features.

3. **Citation context:** Positions our work relative to the theoretical frontier — we use practical approximations (competing token identities, trajectory shape) rather than full spectral decomposition.

## Connection to Other Papers

## Limitations for Our Context

- Too computationally expensive for GEPA's optimization loop
- Requires multiple samples AND matrix decomposition per example
- The practical benefit over simpler methods (entropy, competing tokens) is unclear for prompt optimization
