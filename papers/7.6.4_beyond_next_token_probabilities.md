---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Demonstrates that the **shape of the full distribution** matters, not just the top token probability or entropy. A token probability of 0.1 means fundamentally different things depending on whether the distribution is peaked (one clear winner, 0.1 is the runner-up) or diffused (many tokens around 0.1, no clear winner).

---

## Key Insight: Distribution Shape Features

| Distribution Shape | Top Token P | Entropy | Meaning |
|-------------------|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Peaked** (one dominant token) | 0.85 | Low | Model is confident — single clear answer |
| **Bimodal** (two strong candidates) | 0.45 | Medium | Model torn between two options — binary decision point |
| **Diffuse** (many similar tokens) | 0.08 | High | Model genuinely uncertain — lacks knowledge |
| **Long-tail** (one leader, many weak) | 0.30 | Medium-high | Model has a preference but many alternatives — partially confident |

Entropy alone conflates bimodal (0.45 vs 0.40) with diffuse (0.08 each for 12 tokens) — both have similar entropy but very different diagnostic meaning.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Motivates shape features in the DAL:** Beyond entropy, we should report whether forking tokens have bimodal (two competitors) vs. diffuse (many competitors) distributions. This distinction informs different mutation strategies:
   - **Bimodal:** The model is deciding between two specific alternatives → prompt should clarify which is correct
   - **Diffuse:** The model has no strong preference → prompt needs more foundational guidance

2. **DAL design extension:** Distribution shape features:
   - **Number of modes** (tokens with P > threshold)
   - **Kurtosis** (peakedness)
   - **Gap between top-1 and top-2** (decisive vs. competitive)

3. **Semantic annotation of shape:** Instead of reporting kurtosis values, the DAL should describe the shape semantically:
   - "The model was decisively confident in 'Paris'" (peaked)
   - "The model was torn between exactly two options: 'Paris' and 'London'" (bimodal)
   - "The model had no clear preference among many candidates" (diffuse)

## Implications for Ablations

- **Ablation 1 (representation format):** Shape features should be tested independently. If bimodal vs. diffuse distinction helps but raw entropy doesn't, it validates the shape-over-magnitude principle.
- Supports **H3:** Shape descriptions (semantic) are more informative than entropy values (numerical).
