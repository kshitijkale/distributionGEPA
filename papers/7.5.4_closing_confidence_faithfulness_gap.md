## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Addresses the gap between model confidence and factual correctness — directly relevant to our calibration analysis (Section 5.2). Recent work (2026) that reflects the current state of the confidence-faithfulness literature.

---

## Key Concept

The "confidence-faithfulness gap" refers to the systematic divergence between how confident a model expresses it is and how correct its outputs actually are. This paper proposes methods to close this gap.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Calibration measurement context:** When we measure ECE and Brier scores across our experimental conditions (Section 5.2), this paper provides relevant context for interpreting results.

2. **Secondary metric framing:** If distributional feedback happens to improve the confidence-faithfulness alignment (as a side effect of better prompt optimization), this paper helps us frame that finding.

3. **Does not claim improvement:** Per Section 2.5 of PROJECT_SEED, we measure calibration but do not claim it will improve. This paper is cited for measurement methodology and context, not as a target.
