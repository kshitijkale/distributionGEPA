# How Confident Is the First Token? (UCPOF)

**Section:** 7.8 — Uncertainty-Calibrated Prompt Optimization

**Authors:** Xie et al. (2026)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2603.18009](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18009)

**Venue:** 2026

---

## Why This Paper Is Critical

**Most directly related existing work.** UCPOF uses first-token logprobs with calibration to make prompt-level decisions (selecting few-shot exemplars, triggering RAG). It is the closest published work to our distributional feedback approach, and we must cite and differentiate carefully.

---

## Key Findings

1. **First-token confidence as a decision signal:** The logprob of the first generated token provides a surprisingly good confidence estimate that can guide prompt construction decisions.

2. **+6.03% accuracy improvement:** Using first-token confidence to select few-shot exemplars and decide when to trigger RAG yields significant accuracy gains.

3. **50.66% retrieval reduction:** By only triggering RAG when confidence is low, UCPOF cuts retrieval calls in half while improving accuracy — demonstrating that distributional signals enable efficient resource allocation.

---

## Critical Differentiation from Our Work

| Aspect | UCPOF | Distributional GEPA |
|--------|-------|---------------------|
| **How distributional signals are used** | Prompt *construction* decisions (which examples to include, whether to retrieve) | Prompt *mutation guidance* (what to change in instructions) |
| **When signals are used** | At inference time, per-example | At optimization time, across examples |
| **What changes** | Prompt composition (examples, RAG activation) | Prompt text (instructions evolved via reflection) |
| **Granularity** | Single token (first token) | Full chain (trajectory, forking tokens, answer confidence) |
| **Persistence** | Per-query, ephemeral | Prompt mutations persist across all future queries |

**The approaches are complementary, not competing.** UCPOF optimizes prompt construction; we optimize prompt content. A system could use GEPA-optimized instructions AND UCPOF-guided example selection.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Must cite and differentiate:** This is the paper most likely to be flagged by reviewers as related work. Our differentiation is clear: UCPOF uses distributional signals for runtime decisions; we use them for optimization-time reflection. The mechanisms, timescales, and outputs are fundamentally different.

2. **Validates first-token confidence:** UCPOF's success with just first-token logprobs suggests that even simple distributional features carry actionable information. Our DAL uses richer features (full-chain entropy, competing tokens) but UCPOF shows the principle works.

3. **Baseline consideration:** We could include a UCPOF-style baseline that uses first-token confidence to guide GEPA's minibatch selection (focus on low-confidence examples) rather than enriching the reflective dataset. This would test whether distributional signals help through better example selection (UCPOF-style) or through richer feedback (DAL-style).
