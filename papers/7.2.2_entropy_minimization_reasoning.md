# The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Entropy Minimization in LLM Reasoning

**Section:** 7.2 — Uncertainty in Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

**Authors:** (2025)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2505.15134](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.15134)

**Venue:** 2025

---

## Why This Paper Is Critical

This paper provides theoretical motivation for the entire Distributional GEPA project. It demonstrates that minimizing output entropy — **without any labeled data** — is sufficient to elicit reasoning priors from LLMs.

---

## Key Findings

1. **EM-INF (Entropy Minimization at Inference):** An inference-time method that optimizes logits to minimize output entropy, with no parameter updates and no ground-truth labels.

2. **Entropy contains sufficient optimization signal:** The fact that entropy minimization alone improves reasoning demonstrates that distributional signals carry enough information to guide optimization — even without task labels.

3. **No labeled data required:** EM-INF works purely from the model's own output distribution, suggesting that the distribution encodes the model's "knowledge" about correct reasoning patterns.

---

## Core Argument

If entropy minimization at the logit level (a crude, gradient-based intervention) can improve reasoning without labels, then **entropy-informed prompt optimization** (a more expressive, language-level intervention) should be even more powerful because:
- Prompt changes can make **structural** modifications to how the model approaches a problem
- Prompt changes persist across all examples, not just the current one
- The reflector LLM can interpret **what** the entropy pattern means, not just minimize it blindly

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Theoretical motivation:** This paper is a key citation for arguing that distributional signals are actionable for optimization. If blind entropy minimization works, informed entropy-guided reflection should work better.

2. **Conceptual bridge:** EM-INF operates at the numerical/logit level. Our DAL operates at the semantic/language level. This paper motivates the bridge: translate distributional signals into language (as our DAL does) and let the reflector LLM make more informed decisions than pure entropy minimization could.

3. **Baseline comparison concept:** If EM-INF achieves X% improvement through blind entropy minimization, distributional GEPA should achieve more because the reflector can reason about *why* entropy is high and propose targeted fixes.

---

## Connection to Other Papers

- **Entropy Trajectory Shape (7.2.1):** That paper shows entropy patterns predict correctness; this paper shows entropy minimization improves correctness. Together, they establish entropy as both diagnostic and actionable.
- **Forking Tokens (7.2.3):** EM-INF likely has its biggest effect at forking tokens (high-entropy decision points), connecting to the selective focus of our DAL.
- **ERGO (7.2.5):** Another entropy-guided approach, but at the conversation/prompt level rather than logit level.

---

## Implications for Our Design

- Strengthens the case that distributional information is not just diagnostic but **causally relevant** to reasoning quality
- Suggests that even crude distributional features (overall entropy level) may have value, though our semantic annotations should add more
- The "no labels needed" aspect is less relevant for GEPA (which has labels), but reinforces that entropy carries information beyond what labels provide

---

## Limitations & Open Questions

- EM-INF operates at inference time on individual examples; unclear how its insights transfer to prompt-level optimization across examples
- Entropy minimization could lead to overconfident wrong answers — the same risk applies to distributional feedback if it biases toward low-entropy outputs regardless of correctness
- The mechanism by which entropy minimization helps (sharpening correct answers vs. suppressing noise) may differ from how distributional feedback helps the reflector
