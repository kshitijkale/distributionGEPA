## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Establishes a foundational principle for our work: **contrasts between distributions carry richer information than individual distributions.** This conceptually motivates using distributional diagnostics to identify what's different about good vs. bad model behavior.

---

## Key Concept: Contrastive Decoding

Instead of decoding from a single model's distribution, contrastive decoding:
1. Takes the difference between a strong model's logits and a weak (amateur) model's logits
2. Decodes from this **contrastive distribution**
3. The contrast amplifies the strong model's unique knowledge while suppressing shared biases

```
logits_final = logits_expert - α * logits_amateur
```

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Conceptual motivation:** The principle that distributional contrasts are informative extends to our setting:
   - **Before vs. after prompt mutation:** How does the distribution change when the prompt changes?
   - **Correct vs. incorrect examples:** How do distributional patterns differ between successes and failures?
   - The DAL implicitly provides contrastive information when the reflector sees high-confidence failures vs. low-confidence successes.

2. **Potential extension:** A contrastive DAL feature could compute distributional differences between the parent candidate and the child candidate on the same inputs — showing how the mutation shifted the model's behavior. This is not in our current design but is a natural follow-up.
