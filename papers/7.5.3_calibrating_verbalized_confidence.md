# Calibrating Verbalized Confidence with Self-Generated Distractors

**Section:** 7.5 — Calibration and Verbalized Confidence

**Authors:** Wang et al. (2025)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2509.25532](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.25532)

**Venue:** 2025

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

Improves verbalized confidence calibration by having the model generate plausible alternative answers (distractors) — a technique that further strengthens text-based confidence estimation and thus strengthens the text-enriched baseline (Condition D).

---

## Key Concept

1. After the model generates an answer, ask it to generate plausible alternative answers (distractors)
2. Then ask: "Given these alternatives, how confident are you in your original answer?"
3. The presence of strong distractors lowers confidence; weak distractors maintain it
4. This produces better-calibrated confidence estimates than naive verbalized confidence

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Strengthens Condition D:** This technique could be incorporated into the text-enriched baseline to make it an even stronger control. If Condition D with self-generated distractors matches Condition E, it further challenges the need for logprob-based features.

2. **Conceptual parallel to competing tokens:** Self-generated distractors are a text-level analog of our DAL's competing token identities. The model generates what it might have said instead (text) vs. what the logprobs show it almost said (distributional). If these align, the text approach is sufficient; if they diverge, the distributional approach captures something the model can't articulate.

3. **Calibration measurement:** This technique could improve the calibration quality of all conditions in our experiments, useful for Section 5.2 secondary metrics.
