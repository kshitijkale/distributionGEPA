# Self-Consistency Improves Chain-of-Thought Reasoning in Language Models

**Section:** 7.4 — Self-Consistency and Sampling-Based Confidence

**Authors:** Xuezhi Wang et al.

**Paper:** [arXiv:2203.11171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.11171)

**Venue:** ICLR 2023

---

## Why This Paper Is Essential

Self-consistency is both a **diagnostic feature in our DAL** and a **key component of the text-enriched baseline (Condition D)**. Understanding its strengths and limitations is critical for interpreting our experimental results.

---

## Key Concept: Self-Consistency

1. **Sample diverse reasoning paths:** Given a prompt and input, sample multiple (typically 5-40) chain-of-thought responses at non-zero temperature.
2. **Majority voting:** Select the most frequent final answer across samples.
3. **Confidence estimation:** The frequency of the majority answer serves as a confidence score (e.g., 4 out of 5 samples agree → 80% confidence).

---

## Key Results

- **+17.9% absolute improvement on GSM8K** over single-sample CoT
- Consistent improvements across multiple benchmarks and model sizes
- Works with any CoT-capable model — no special training required

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

### In the DAL (optional feature)
Self-consistency can be included as a distributional feature:
- **Agreement rate:** "3 out of 5 samples agreed on 'Paris'"
- **Divergence point:** "The dissenting samples diverged at step 3, choosing different source documents"
- **Cost:** 2-3x more LLM calls per example (expensive)
- Only used if rollout budget allows; may be reserved for high-value diagnostics

### In the Text-Enriched Baseline (Condition D)
Self-consistency results presented as text:
- "2 out of 3 runs agreed on 'Paris'; the third said 'London' and diverged at the document selection step"
- This is a TEXT-ONLY uncertainty signal — no logprobs needed
- Makes Condition D a strong baseline that captures uncertainty without distributional access

### In Ablation 3 (self-consistency as substitute)
Key experimental question: **Can self-consistency substitute for logprobs on API-only models?**
- If self-consistency (no logprobs) provides most of the improvement, the DAL's logprob dependency is unnecessary
- If logprob-based features significantly outperform self-consistency, the DAL's distributional approach is justified

---

## Critical Implication: The Condition D Threat

Self-consistency is arguably the **strongest threat** to our distributional hypothesis. It provides:
- Uncertainty information (agreement rate)
- Diagnostic information (where reasoning paths diverge)
- No logprob requirement

If Condition D (text-enriched, using self-consistency) matches Condition E (distributional, using logprobs), our contribution shifts from "distributional signals help" to "any uncertainty enrichment helps" — a less novel finding.

**Counter-argument:** Self-consistency is 2-3x more expensive per example than logprob extraction, and provides coarser information (agreement on final answer, not per-token uncertainty). Logprob-based features may be both cheaper and more informative.

## Key Numbers for Cost Analysis

- Cost must be tracked for fair accuracy-vs-total-compute comparisons (Section 5.4)
