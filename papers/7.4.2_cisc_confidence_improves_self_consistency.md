# Confidence Improves Self-Consistency in LLMs (CISC)

**Section:** 7.4 — Self-Consistency and Sampling-Based Confidence

**Authors:** (ACL 2025)

**Paper:** [aclanthology.org/2025.findings-acl.1030.pdf](https://aclanthology.org/2025.findings-acl.1030.pdf)

**Venue:** ACL 2025 (Findings)

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

CISC demonstrates that combining P(True)-weighted voting with self-consistency achieves the **same accuracy with 46% fewer samples**. This motivates combining logprob-based and sampling-based signals in our DAL.

---

## Key Findings

1. **P(True)-weighted voting:** Instead of uniform majority voting over samples, weight each sample's vote by the model's P(True) confidence (probability assigned to the statement "this answer is correct").

2. **46% fewer samples for same accuracy:** Weighted voting extracts more information per sample, dramatically reducing the number of samples needed.

3. **Synergy between logprobs and sampling:** The combination of distributional signals (P(True) logprob) and sampling-based signals (self-consistency) is more effective than either alone.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Motivates combining signals:** CISC shows that logprob-based confidence and sampling-based consistency are complementary, not redundant. This supports including both in the DAL when budget allows.

2. **Efficiency argument:** If CISC can reduce self-consistency cost by 46% using logprobs, our DAL's logprob features may similarly reduce the number of examples needed for effective reflection — supporting the sample efficiency hypothesis.

3. **Feature design:** P(True)-weighted confidence could be a DAL feature for the anchor token analysis component.

## Connection to Other Papers

## Limitations

- P(True) requires an additional model call ("Is this answer correct?") or logprob access
- The 46% sample reduction may not translate directly to GEPA's optimization budget savings
