# LogitScope: A Framework for Analyzing LLM Uncertainty Through Information Metrics

**Section:** 7.9 — Practical Tools and Infrastructure

**Authors:** (2026)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2603.24929](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24929)

**Venue:** 2026

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

A lightweight framework specifically for **token-level entropy and varentropy computation** — potentially more suitable than LM-Polygraph for our specific needs since it focuses on exactly the metrics we need for the DAL.

---

## Key Features

1. **Token-level entropy computation:** Efficient Shannon entropy calculation from logprobs at each token position.

2. **Varentropy (variance of entropy):** Measures how much entropy varies across token positions — related to our entropy trajectory analysis.

3. **Lightweight:** Focused on information-theoretic metrics rather than the full range of uncertainty methods in LM-Polygraph.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Potential entropy computation engine:** If LM-Polygraph adds too much overhead or doesn't integrate well with vLLM, LogitScope provides a lighter-weight alternative specifically designed for token-level entropy analysis.

2. **Varentropy as a feature:** Varentropy (entropy of entropy) could be a useful DAL feature:
   - Low varentropy + high entropy → uniformly uncertain (knowledge gap)
   - High varentropy → entropy spikes at specific points (forking tokens)
   - Low varentropy + low entropy → uniformly confident (reliable or confidently wrong)

3. **Implementation reference:** Even if we don't use LogitScope directly, its codebase provides implementation reference for efficient entropy computation.
