# ERGO: Entropy-Guided Resetting for Generation Optimization

**Section:** 7.2 — Uncertainty in Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

**Authors:** (2025)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2510.14077](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14077)

**Venue:** 2025

---

## Why This Paper Is Relevant

ERGO validates entropy monitoring for **prompt-level decisions** at inference time. While the Entropy-Guided Loop (7.2.4) operates on single generations, ERGO operates across multi-turn conversations — closer to GEPA's iterative optimization setting.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Entropy spike detection in conversations:** ERGO monitors Shannon entropy during multi-turn interactions and detects when entropy spikes above a baseline.

2. **Adaptive prompt consolidation:** When entropy spikes are detected, ERGO triggers a "reset" — consolidating the conversation context into a cleaner prompt to reduce accumulated confusion.

3. **56.6% average performance gain:** This significant improvement demonstrates that entropy-triggered prompt interventions are highly effective.

---

## Core Mechanism

1. Track per-turn entropy during multi-turn generation
2. When entropy exceeds a threshold (indicating model confusion or context degradation):
   - Consolidate the conversation into a fresh, cleaner prompt
   - Reset the generation from this consolidated state
3. Continue generation with lower entropy / more focused reasoning

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Validates entropy monitoring for prompt-level decisions:** ERGO shows that entropy signals can drive effective prompt modifications (consolidation/rewriting), not just decoding adjustments. We extend this from inference time to optimization time.

2. **Entropy as a trigger:** ERGO uses entropy as a binary trigger (spike vs. no spike). Our DAL provides richer annotation (trajectory shape, forking token identities, confidence levels), but the core principle — entropy signals → prompt action — is the same.

3. **Multi-turn context:** ERGO's multi-turn setting is relevant to compound AI systems with multiple LLM calls. Entropy could spike at different modules in a GEPA-optimized system, and the DAL could diagnose which module's context is degrading.

---

## Connection to Other Papers

- **Entropy Trajectory Shape (7.2.1):** ERGO uses entropy spikes as triggers; the trajectory paper characterizes the full shape. Both show temporal entropy patterns matter.
- **Entropy-Guided Loop (7.2.4):** Single-generation refinement vs. multi-turn conversation resetting. ERGO operates at a coarser granularity.
- **Reinforcement Inference (7.2.6):** Also uses entropy as a trigger for corrective action.

---

## Limitations for Our Context

- ERGO's "resetting" mechanism is specific to multi-turn conversations; doesn't directly transfer to single-turn CoT optimization
- The 56.6% gain is on a specific multi-turn setting — gains may be smaller for our single-turn tasks
- The binary trigger (spike vs. no spike) may be less informative than our richer diagnostic annotations
