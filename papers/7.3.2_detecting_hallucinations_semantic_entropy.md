# Detecting Hallucinations in Large Language Models Using Semantic Entropy

**Section:** 7.3 — Semantic Entropy and Meaning-Level Uncertainty

**Authors:** Sebastian Farquhar, Jannik Kossen, Lorenz Kuhn, Yarin Gal (Oxford)

**Paper:** [nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07421-0](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07421-0)

**Venue:** Nature, 2024

---

## Why This Paper Is Essential

Published in **Nature** — the highest-profile validation of distributional uncertainty methods for LLMs. Extends semantic entropy to hallucination detection, providing the strongest evidence that distributional signals carry actionable information about LLM output quality.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Semantic entropy detects hallucinations:** High semantic entropy reliably indicates that a model is hallucinating (generating plausible-sounding but incorrect content).

2. **Better than token-level methods:** Semantic entropy outperforms simple token probability or perplexity-based hallucination detection because it distinguishes genuine uncertainty from phrasing variation.

3. **Nature-level validation:** The fact that this passed Nature's review bar establishes distributional uncertainty analysis as a rigorous, impactful research direction.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **High-authority citation:** When motivating why distributional signals matter for LLM optimization, this Nature paper provides the strongest supporting evidence.

2. **Hallucination as a failure mode:** The DAL can identify cases where the model hallucinated with high confidence (low entropy on an incorrect answer) — a failure mode that text-only traces might not reveal if the hallucination is internally consistent.

3. **Semantic clustering concept:** While we don't implement full semantic entropy (too expensive), the insight that meaning-level clustering matters informs how we design competing token annotations — we focus on semantically distinct alternatives, not trivial variations.

## Connection to Other Papers

- Direct extension of **Semantic Uncertainty (7.3.1)** to a practical application
- **Semantic Entropy Probes (7.3.3):** Shows you can approximate this from hidden states cheaply
- **CCS (7.6.3):** Both papers demonstrate that internal model signals encode truth/quality information beyond what text outputs show

---

## Limitations for Our Context

- Requires multiple samples per input — expensive for GEPA's budget
- Hallucination detection is binary (hallucinating or not), while we need richer diagnostic information (where, why, and how to fix)
- The method is designed for detection, not optimization feedback
