# Entropy Trajectory Shape Predicts LLM Reasoning Reliability

**Section:** 7.2 — Uncertainty in Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

**Authors:** (2025)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2603.18940](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18940)

**Venue:** 2025

---

## Why This Paper Is Critical

This paper DIRECTLY supports the core mechanism of the Distributional GEPA project. It provides the foundational empirical evidence that distributional signals (specifically entropy trajectory shape) carry diagnostic information about reasoning quality.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Shape-over-magnitude dissociation:** Whether per-step entropy monotonically decreases during chain-of-thought reasoning predicts accuracy far better than absolute entropy values.
   - **Monotone decreasing entropy → 68.8% accuracy**
   - **Non-monotone entropy → 46.8% accuracy**
   - **p = 0.0005** — highly statistically significant

2. **The pattern matters, not the value:** A reasoning chain where entropy goes [2.1, 1.5, 0.8, 0.3] (monotonically decreasing) is more likely correct than one where entropy goes [0.5, 2.3, 0.4, 0.2] (non-monotone with a spike), even though the latter has lower average entropy.

3. **Computational efficiency:** Entropy trajectory analysis costs approximately **1/8 the compute of self-consistency** (which requires 3-5+ samples), making it a cheap diagnostic signal.

---

## Core Concept: Entropy Trajectory

For a chain-of-thought with N reasoning steps:
- Compute Shannon entropy at each step: H_1, H_2, ..., H_N
- A trajectory is **monotone** if H_1 ≥ H_2 ≥ ... ≥ H_N (the model becomes progressively more certain)
- A trajectory is **non-monotone** if there exists any i where H_i < H_{i+1} (the model's uncertainty increases at some point)

Non-monotone trajectories indicate the model encountered something unexpected or contradictory mid-reasoning — a strong signal that the reasoning may be unreliable.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Entropy trajectory monotonicity as a Pareto dimension:** Use the fraction of evaluation examples with monotonically decreasing entropy as an objective in GEPA's Pareto frontier (using the `"objective"` or `"hybrid"` frontier type). This creates selection pressure toward prompts that produce more reliable reasoning.

2. **Trajectory annotations in the DAL diagnostic report:** The Distributional Annotation Layer classifies each execution's entropy trajectory (monotone vs. non-monotone, where spikes occur) and includes this in the structured natural language annotation.

3. **Evidence for the distributional information gap (H1):** This paper demonstrates that distributional signals carry diagnostic information that is NOT visible in the sampled text. A text trace might look perfectly reasonable even when the entropy trajectory is non-monotone — the model "recovered" from its uncertainty spike and produced plausible-looking text.

4. **Support for H3 (representation matters):** The shape-over-magnitude finding directly supports our hypothesis that qualitative/semantic descriptions ("confidence dropped at step 3 then recovered") are more informative than numerical reports ("H=2.31 bits").

## Relevance to Experimental Design

- **Phase 1, Experiment 1.1:** We attempt to replicate this finding on HotpotQA with Llama 3 70B via vLLM. If the monotone vs. non-monotone accuracy gap does not replicate with at least a 10pp difference, we reconsider the approach.
- **Ablation 1 (representation format):** "Trajectory shape only" is one of the ablation conditions — testing whether monotone/non-monotone classification alone (without per-token details) provides sufficient diagnostic value.

## Limitations & Open Questions

- The 68.8% vs. 46.8% gap was demonstrated on specific tasks/models — may not generalize to all settings
- How to segment "reasoning steps" for trajectory analysis is task-dependent
- The threshold for "monotone" is binary — there may be value in finer-grained trajectory shape classification (U-shaped, oscillating, etc.)
