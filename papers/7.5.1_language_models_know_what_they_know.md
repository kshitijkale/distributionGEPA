# Language Models (Mostly) Know What They Know

**Section:** 7.5 — Calibration and Verbalized Confidence

**Authors:** Saurav Kadavath et al. (Anthropic)

**Paper:** [arXiv:2207.05221](https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.05221)

**Venue:** arXiv, 2022

---

## Why This Paper Is Essential

Foundational work on **LLM self-knowledge and metacognition**. Establishes that LLMs have meaningful metacognitive abilities — they can estimate their own confidence — which is the prerequisite for our entire approach (feeding distributional self-information back to a reflector).

---

## Key Findings

1. **P(True) provides useful confidence estimates:** When asked "Is the following statement true?", models assign higher probabilities to actually-true statements. This P(True) score is a useful confidence measure.

2. **Temperature adjustment fixes RLHF miscalibration:** RLHF training distorts logprob calibration, but temperature scaling (T=2.5) largely restores it. This means raw logprobs from RLHF'd models need recalibration before being interpreted as confidence.

3. **Metacognitive ability established:** Models can distinguish what they know from what they don't — not perfectly, but reliably enough to be useful.

---

## How We Use It in Distributional GEPA

1. **Foundation for distributional feedback:** If models "know what they know," then their output distributions encode this knowledge. The DAL extracts and presents this self-knowledge to the reflector.

2. **Calibration awareness:** RLHF-distorted logprobs are a known issue. Our DAL should:
   - Use **relative** comparisons (competing token ratios) rather than absolute probabilities
   - Consider temperature scaling for open-weight models
   - Be aware that API models may have additional calibration issues

3. **P(True) as a feature:** The P(True) paradigm could be incorporated into the text-enriched baseline (Condition D) — ask the model "How confident are you?" after generation.

---

## Critical Implication: RLHF Distortion

**RLHF training distorts logprob calibration.** This means:
- Raw logprobs from RLHF'd models (including most models we test with) may be miscalibrated
- Entropy values computed from uncalibrated logprobs may be noisy
- **Competing token identities** (which tokens compete, not their exact probabilities) may be more robust than probability values
- This supports H3 (semantic > numerical representation) and our semantic-first DAL format

---

## Connection to Other Papers

- **Just Ask for Calibration (7.5.2):** Extends this finding — verbalized confidence is often *better* calibrated than logprobs for RLHF'd models. This is a threat to our approach.
- **Self-Consistency (7.4.1):** Another way to estimate model confidence without relying on (potentially miscalibrated) logprobs
- **CISC (7.4.2):** Uses P(True) for weighted self-consistency voting
- **Know When You're Wrong (7.5.5):** Trains models to align confidence with correctness
